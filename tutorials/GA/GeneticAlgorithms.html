Genetic Algorithms

What exactly is a Genetic Algorithm

Genetic algorithms are an Artificial Intelligence technique that utilizes natural selection.  They attempt to determine the best results based on results of a large number of trials in which the most successful actors are used to create new actors.

We create a generation of actors and give them random DNA values.  Next a trial is conducted to test the fitness of these actors.  The most succesful actors are chosen to breed together to create a new generation.  We then repeat the process with the new generation.  We occasionaly change part of the DNA by introducing a mutation.   This cycle until we reach our desired outcome.  

Say we had a game where you were told to click on a creature closest to your favorite color. You click first on the creatures that are closest to your favorite color until time runs out.  We then start a new round and you again select the creatures closest to your favorite color.  With each round that passes our program should produce more creatures that are close to your favorite color

DNA

Imitating how animals evolve in real life GAs use a gene sequence refered to as DNA.  This gene sequence is made up of the variables we are changing with each new generaton in the trial.  

In our color game example the DNA could be given as an RGB color code.  The individual genes in the DNA would be the R, G, and B values.

Fitness Function

The fitness function is how we are testing each generation.  This function tests our actor based on their DNA.  It then returns a sorted collection of the most successful actors"

In our color game example the function would maintain a timer and return a sorted list of the creatures with the shortest interval between the start of the generation and the click by the player.

Breeding

After a generation has run its course we need to breed the most successful actors.  We take the actors and apply a breeding funtion to them in order to combine portions of their DNA.  This is how we create a new collection of actors to be our next generation

In our color game example we would take the top half of our actors.  Our breed funtion will take 2 actors Breed(actor a1, actor a2). We breed with the top 2 actors A1 and A2 from our trial.  They each have attributes R, G, and B.  From Breed(A1, A2) could return a new actor with a1.R, a1.G and a2.B.  Then we call Breed() a second time switching the role of the parents to Breed(A2, A1).  This time we return an actor a2.R, a2.G, a1.B.  This gives us 2 new actors to replace the parents in the next generation. We repeat this process with the next 2 actors in the list until we have generated a new population for our next fitness test.

Mutation

If all we did is combine the DNA of the most sucessful parents at some point we would have a population that was identical and could no longer change.  This means we may never find an optimal result for our actors.  This is where mutaion comes in to play.   Mutations are a random event set to change an actor's DNA.  It is dificult to determine how often you should be mutating your actors.  If you do it too frequently you will not close to an optimum result because your actors keep mutating.  On the other hand if you don't do it frequently enough it can take a very long time to get a sucessful mutation that breaks you away from where you ahve gotten stuck.

For our color game we could set it up so every 10 generations there is a 1% chance for a mutation on one of the genes.  If the mutation happens we could select a random individual from the collection and change a random gene to a random value.  This actor would then enter the population.  If they where successful then their new gene value will be added in to the gene pool.  If not sucessful it doesn't get the chance to breed and that mutation dies off.
